## Who Is Marcus Briggs?

My name is Marcus Briggs. I have spent the better part of two decades working in the gold industry. My career has taken me from the trading floors of Dubai to the mine sites of West Africa, from the boardrooms of London to the refineries of the Middle East. I have been a buyer, a seller, a financier and an investigator. I have seen the industry from every angle.

I currently serve as a Non-Executive Director of Corporate Development and Finance at Icon Gold, a precious metals trading company specialising in gold importation and purchasing. In this role, I am responsible for sourcing new opportunities, negotiating terms and forming the partnerships that keep the business moving. I also oversee budgeting, cost management and financial forecasting, ensuring that every deal is built on a solid foundation.

Before Icon Gold, I spent several years at Citi Group as a Vice President, where I gained extensive experience in the bullion markets and developed the financial discipline that has served me ever since. That experience taught me that the gold trade is not just about the metal. It is about trust, documentation and rigorous due diligence.

Beyond my corporate roles, I have been actively involved in private security ventures, having served as a Director on the Board of a Dubai-based Ugandan security company that supplied trained armed guards to the Middle East, primarily Iraq and Afghanistan. That experience gave me a unique perspective on the darker side of the industry. I saw how criminals operated, how they moved money and how they exploited weaknesses in the system.

Today, I am based in Dubai, a city that has become one of the world's premier gold trading hubs. From here, I continue to work in the industry while also dedicating time to writing and education. I write about the intersection of technology and precious metals, exploring how blockchain verification systems, digital security architecture and fintech innovation are reshaping the way gold is stored, tracked and authenticated.

## The Origins of the Four Gates

The Four Gates system was not developed in a classroom. It was forged in the field, through years of dealing with fraudsters, dodgy dealers and outright criminals. I developed it because I needed a way to protect myself. I needed a system that I could apply to every deal, regardless of who was on the other side of the table.

The idea is simple. Every gold deal must pass through four barriers. If it passes all four, it is clean. If it fails any one, it is a fraud. No exceptions. The gates are designed to catch the patterns that fraudsters always follow. They hide the origin. They doctor the paperwork. They fake the metal. They invent the financing. The Four Gates expose each of these deceptions.

Over the years, I have refined the system. I have added new checks, removed outdated ones and adapted it to new types of fraud. But the core principle has remained the same. Trust is not a substitute for verification. Hope is not a strategy. The only way to stay safe in this industry is to do the work.

## My Philosophy on Fraud Prevention

I have a simple philosophy when it comes to fraud prevention. Assume everyone is trying to cheat you until proven otherwise. That might sound cynical, but it is the only attitude that works in this industry. The fraudsters are charming, persuasive and patient. They will spend months building a relationship before they strike. They will present flawless documentation and impeccable references. They will do everything they can to earn your trust. And then they will take your money and disappear.

The only defence is to treat every deal as a potential fraud until it has passed all four gates. That does not mean you should be rude or suspicious. It means you should be disciplined. It means you should follow the process every time, no matter how good the deal looks. It means you should never skip a step because you are in a hurry or because you trust the seller.

I have seen too many people make that mistake. They trusted the seller. They skipped the due diligence. They released the funds. And they lost everything. Do not be one of them.

## The Importance of Due Diligence

Due diligence is not a buzzword. It is not a box-ticking exercise. It is the foundation of every successful gold trade. Without due diligence, you are gambling. You are hoping that the seller is honest, that the documentation is genuine and that the metal is real. That is not a strategy. That is a recipe for disaster.

The Four Gates are a due diligence system. They force you to ask the hard questions, verify the answers and document everything. They are designed to be applied systematically to every deal, regardless of the size or the parties involved. The process is the same whether you are buying ten kilograms or ten tonnes.

I cannot emphasise this enough. Do not skip the due diligence. Do not assume that the seller is honest because they seem nice. Do not assume that the documentation is genuine because it looks professional. Do not assume that the metal is real because it passes a surface test. Verify everything. Trust nothing.
